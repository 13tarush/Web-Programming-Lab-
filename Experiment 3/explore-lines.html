Mumbai’s suburban railway network is divided into major lines that serve different regions of the city. Below is an overview of the three major railway lines: Western, Central, and Harbour.

Line descriptions on this page are styled using internal CSS.

Western Line

The Western Line runs along the western coast of Mumbai and is one of the busiest suburban railway corridors.

Route: Churchgate → Borivali

Major Stations:

  • Churchgate
  • Mumbai Central
  • Dadar (Interchange)
  • Bandra (Interchange)
  • Andheri
  • Borivali
View Western Line Route

Central Line

The Central Line connects South Mumbai to the central and eastern suburbs, serving key residential and commercial areas.

Route: CSMT → Thane

Major Stations:

  • CSMT
  • Dadar (Interchange)
  • Kurla (Interchange)
  • Ghatkopar (Interchange)
  • Mulund
  • Thane
View Central Line Route

Harbour Line

The Harbour Line serves eastern and Navi Mumbai regions and provides important connectivity through multiple interchange stations.

Route: CSMT → Panvel

Major Stations:

  • CSMT
  • Vadala Road (Interchange)
  • Kurla (Interchange)
  • Chembur
  • Vashi
  • Panvel
